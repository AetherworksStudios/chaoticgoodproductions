About Us

Chaotic Good Productions is a New York–based independent production company that develops and produces original TV series and feature films. We strive to entertain while also telling stories that matter.

Our latest projects include a TV pilot for ADVERSARY, a woman-led gritty psychological thriller set in the 90’s about the dark side of the music industry (now in pre-production), plus two series in development— SPEDZ (an animated superhero saga) and MOMMYLAND (a dark comedy on misogyny and the dangers of self-proclaimed life coaches).

Adversary: A gritty, psychological thriller TV pilot.

In 1994 two volatile women are forced to collaborate on a reality show that will either save their careers or destroy their lives—all while an unseen predator closes in.

SPEDZ: A 2D animated action, mystery, comedy TV pilot.

A hyperactive go-getter, an anxious clairvoyant, a clinically depressed basket case, and an artist with autism journey on a cross-country road trip to solve mysterious online puzzles that promise the winners a dream school that supports kids with special needs... and special super powers.

MOMMYLAND: A psychological dark comedy TV pilot.

A scam artist turned life coach for disenfranchised, incel men, creates a false philosophy that the root of all problems are their moms, and convinces them to embark on a dangerous journey to confront them, at a deadly cost.

Our Team

CEO: Jon Klein is an experienced director, writer, and one of five Americans who founded MTV Europe in the 90's. He directed music videos for artists including U2, Oasis, and Sinéad O'Connor, and was the Co-Creator and Director for Buzz, MTV's first internationally broadcast series. After a 20 year hiatus from the industry to focus on his family, he rekindled his passion for film and TV, and founded the indie production company Chaotic Good Productions to bring fresh and relevant TV series to a modern audience. Co-Writer and Production Coordinator: Sabrina Z. Geffner is a writer, director, casting director, and production coordinator. As a woman in TV, she’s driven to write stories that speak about authentic female experiences, and shed light onto diverse perspectives like ADVERSARY and SPEDZ. Most recently she wrote and directed a NYIFA and Count Basie BREAKTHROUGH award-winning drama short, GOOD ENOUGH (11 mins) about musicians yearning for parental approval and the search for self-worth. In her free time she likes to sew costumes, attend cosplay conventions, and renaissance fares.
